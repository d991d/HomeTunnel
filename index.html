Setup Guide

Simple step-by-step instructions — no technical knowledge needed.

1

Download HomeTunnel Server for your computer

Use the download buttons above to get the right version for your Mac, Windows, or Linux machine. You are the gateway — your computer's internet connection is what your friend will use.

2

Run the app

Mac: Double-click Launch HomeTunnel.command. If macOS says it can't be opened, right-click it → Open → Open anyway. Enter your Mac password when asked — this is needed to set up the secure tunnel.

Windows: Right-click hometunnel-server.exe → Run as administrator.

Linux: Open Terminal and run sudo ./hometunnel-server.

⚠️ Keep the app running whenever your friend wants to connect. You don't need to do anything else — it runs quietly in the background.

3

Forward port 48321 on your router

Log into your home router (usually at 192.168.1.1 or 192.168.0.1), find the "Port Forwarding" section, and add a rule: UDP port 48321 → your computer's local IP.

Your computer's local IP is shown in the HomeTunnel dashboard. If you're not sure how to do this, search "port forwarding [your router brand]".

4

Generate an invite link for your friend

Open the HomeTunnel dashboard in your browser at http://localhost:7777. Click "New Invite", enter your friend's name, and click Generate. You'll see a link that looks like:

hometunnel://your.ip:48321?token=...

Send this link to your friend via any messaging app (WhatsApp, Telegram, email, etc.).

⚠️ Each invite link expires after 72 hours by default. You can generate a new one anytime from the dashboard.

1

Download HomeTunnel for your device

Download the Android APK above, or wait for the iOS App Store release. On Android, you'll need to allow installing apps from outside the Play Store — go to Settings → Security → Install unknown apps and allow your browser or file manager.

2

Ask your friend for an invite link

Your friend (the server host) needs to generate a link for you from their HomeTunnel dashboard. The link looks like hometunnel://.... They can send it to you via any messaging app.

3

Tap the link to connect

Tap the invite link in your messaging app. HomeTunnel will open automatically and pre-fill all the connection details. Tap Connect.

Android will ask for VPN permission — tap OK. This is normal and required for the VPN to work.

You're connected! All your internet traffic now routes through your friend's home connection. You can browse freely.

4

Disconnect when done

Open HomeTunnel and tap Disconnect, or pull down the notification and tap Disconnect there. Your connection will return to normal.

💡 You can leave HomeTunnel connected all the time — it automatically reconnects if the connection drops.

Frequently Asked Questions

Plain-language answers to common questions.

Is this safe to use?

Yes. All traffic between you and your friend's computer is encrypted using the same modern cryptography that banks use (ChaCha20 encryption with X25519 key exchange). Nobody — not even your internet provider or government — can read what you're doing. The only person who can see your traffic is the friend running the server, just as if you were using their Wi-Fi.

Does the server host see my internet traffic?

Technically, your traffic exits through their home internet connection — the same as if you were physically at their house using their Wi-Fi. This is why you should only use HomeTunnel with someone you genuinely trust: a close friend or family member.

Will this slow down my internet?

Your speed depends on your friend's home internet upload speed. Most home connections have enough bandwidth for browsing, video calls, and streaming without noticeable slowdown. If your friend has a fast connection (100+ Mbps upload), you likely won't notice any difference.

Do I need to set it up every time?

The server host needs to keep the app running, but you as the client just tap the invite link once — or reconnect with a single tap. HomeTunnel remembers your last connection, so you never need to enter the link again after the first time.

What if the invite link stops working?

Invite links expire after 72 hours by default for security reasons. If your link has expired, ask your friend to generate a new one from their HomeTunnel dashboard. The whole process takes about 10 seconds on their end.

Is this legal?

HomeTunnel is a privacy tool, not a tool for illegal activity. Using a VPN is legal in most countries, though some governments try to restrict VPN use as part of their censorship efforts. You are responsible for your own use. HomeTunnel itself does not facilitate anything beyond bypassing content restrictions.

Can I use this on my phone and computer at the same time?

Yes — your friend can generate separate invite links for each of your devices. Each device connects independently. The server supports up to 253 simultaneous connections.

Does the friend need a special internet connection?

No. Any standard home internet connection works. The only requirement is that they forward UDP port 48321 on their router — a one-time, 5-minute setup. The HomeTunnel dashboard shows them exactly how to do it.
